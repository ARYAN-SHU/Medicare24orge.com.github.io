1. COVID-19

The COVID-19 or coronavirus is one of the top 10 deadliest infectious diseases globally. This disease has hit the world in 2019 and affect many people. Nearly 5.24 lakh people in India have lost their lives in the past two years. In 2020, the government of India has launched two COVID-19 health insurance policies (Corona Kavach and Corona Rakshak) that will cover your entire medical and hospitalization expenses. The disease is transmitted from an infected person (through droplets when he coughs, sneezes, or speaks) to others. Though this virus produces mild respiratory illness, it can be severe in people who have cardiovascular issues, diabetes and chronic respiratory diseases.

Symptoms of COVID-19

  • Severe cold
  • Headache
  • Fever
  • Body Pain
  • Loss of taste and smell
  • Scratchy throat

Preventive Measures of COVID-19

  • Vaccination
  • Follow a healthy diet
  • Wash your hands frequently
  • Maintain social distancing
  • Wear a mask while going outside

2. Cardiovascular Diseases (CVD)

Cardiovascular diseases affect your heart and they are one of the pivotal causes of death in India.

Symptoms of Cardiovascular Diseases

  • Shortness of
  • Chest pain and discomfort
  • Pain in the upper abdomen, neck, back, throat, jaw
  • Pain or numbness in your arms, and legs

Causes of Cardiovascular Diseases

  • Excessive smoking

3. Diabetes

This is one of the common diseases in humans. Two types of diabetes are there (type 1 and type 2). In type 1 diabetes, your pancreas doesn’t produce adequate insulin. In type 2 diabetes, insulin produced by your body is not effectively utilized.




figure2.5

Symptoms of Diabetes

  • Frequent urge to urination
  • Enhanced hunger and thirst
  • Weight loss
  • Extreme fatigue

Preventive Measures for Diabetes

  • Do physical activities
  • Follow a healthy diet
  • Reduce your carbohydrate intake
  • Quit smoking and restrict alcohol consumption
  • Monitor your blood glucose level

4. Respiratory Diseases

This disease may lead to death, especially in children. Asthma, tuberculosis, bronchitis, pneumonia, etc., are some of the most common respiratory diseases. Air pollution is one of the prime causes of respiratory diseases.




figure2.6

Causes of Respiratory Diseases

  • Excessive exposure to toxic substances
  • Dust
  • Air pollution
  • Smoking
  • Lower immunity levels

Symptoms of Respiratory Diseases

  • Cough
  • Sneezing
  • Nasal congestion
  • Fever
  • Shortness of breath

Preventive Measures for Respiratory Diseases

  • If you need to travel in polluted areas, cover your face
  • Quit smoking
  • To enhance your immunity level, take vitamins and minerals
  • Do deep breathing exercises

7. Alzheimer’s Disease and Other Dementias

Though in Alzheimer’s you may lose your memory, in some cases, your life is also threatened.




figure2.9

Symptoms of Alzheimer’s Disease

  • Difficulty in accomplishing daily tasks
  • Misplacing things
  • Problems with words in writing or speaking
  • Inferior judgment and decision making
  • Inability to manage budget

Causes of Alzheimer’s Disease

  • Family history
  • Old age
  • Unwholesome lifestyle
  • Previous head trauma
  • Leading an unsocial life for a long period

Preventive Measures for Alzheimer’s Disease

  • Avoid fatty food (Meat, dairy products, olive oil, lean fish, etc.)
  • Follow a heart-healthy diet (incorporate fruits and veggies in your diet)
  • Discard smoking
  • Control of cardiovascular risk factors

8. Malaria

Malaria is one of the common diseases in India which is caused by Plasmodium parasite transmission by mosquitoes.




figure2.10

Symptoms of Malaria

  • Fever
  • Muscle ache
  • Nausea
  • Abdominal pain
  • Headache
  • Diarrhea

Preventive Measures for Malaria

  • Use insect repellent
  • Wear full-sleeved clothes while going
  • Use bed net while sleeping
  • Eat food items like carrot, papaya, beetroots, fruits especially citrus fruits

9. Diarrheal Diseases

Diarrhea happens when you pass loos e stools more than three times in a day. This minimizes the water and salt levels in your body and makes you weak. If it continues for several days , you may experience dehydration.




figure2.11

Symptoms of Diarrheal Disease

  • Watery stool
  • Mucus in the stool
  • Bloating
  • Nausea
  • Abdominal pain, cramps
  • Blood in the stool

Causes of Diarrheal Diseases

  • Unhygienic water
  • Areas with poor sanitation facilities
  • Malnourishment
  • Inferior immune system
